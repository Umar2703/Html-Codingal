Fun facts about association football (soccer)

  • Fastest red card: Lee Todd was given a red card just two seconds into a game in 2000 for shouting "f--- me, that was loud" after the referee's whistle blew.

  • Dog finds the trophy: The Jules Rimet World Cup trophy was stolen in England in 1966 but was found a week later by a dog named Pickles, wrapped in newspaper under a bush.

  • Highest-scoring game: The highest-scoring professional soccer game on record was a 149–0 match in Madagascar in 2002. One team intentionally scored 149 own goals to protest referee decisions.

  • No barefoot players: India withdrew from the 1950 World Cup because FIFA refused to let their team play barefoot.

  • Smallest professional footballer: At just 5 feet tall, Marcin Garuch is one of the smallest professional footballers in the world.

  • Origins in China: The earliest form of a kicking game for which there is historical evidence is cuju, played in China as early as the 2nd century BC.

  • home loss: Chelsea manager José Mourinho once went 150 home games over nine years without a single home defeat across various clubs.

  • First televised match: The first televised soccer game was a practice match between Arsenal and their reserves in 1937.

  • No games on a pitch: The English FA banned women from playing on official pitches from 1921 to 1971, claiming the sport was "quite unsuitable for females".

  • The floating field: The Float at Marina Bay in Singapore is the world's largest floating soccer field. Goalkeeper goal record: Brazilian goalkeeper Rogério Ceni scored 131 career goals, mostly from free kicks and penalties, making him the highest-scoring goalkeeper of all time.

  • Soccer or football?: The term "soccer" originated in England in the 1880s as a slang abbreviation for "association" (football).
