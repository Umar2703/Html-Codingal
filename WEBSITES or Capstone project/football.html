About soccer: The world's most popular sport

Soccer, or association football, is a team sport played by two teams of 11 players on a rectangular field called a pitch. The objective is to score more goals than the opposing team by moving the ball into their goal, primarily using feet, the head, or the torso. It is the most popular sport globally, with an estimated 250 million players in over 200 countries.


History and governance

Origin: The modern version of soccer originated in England in the 19th century. The Football Association was formed in 1863 to standardize the rules of the game. International body: The sport is now governed globally by the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA). World Cup: FIFA oversees international competitions, most notably the men's World Cup, first held in 1930, and the women's World Cup, which began in 1991.


Basic rules and gameplay

Game time: A professional match consists of two 45-minute halves, with "stoppage time" added by the referee at the end of each half to account for delays. A 15-minute halftime break separates the two halves. Scoring: The team that scores the most goals wins. Depending on the competition, a tied score at the end of regulation can result in a draw, extra time, or a penalty shoot-out. No hands or arms: With the exception of the goalkeeper, players are not allowed to use their hands or arms to touch the ball. Fouls: Infractions result in free kicks, with more serious fouls within the penalty area resulting in a penalty kick for the attacking team. Cards: The referee uses yellow cards as a caution for misconduct and a red card for serious fouls, which results in ejection from the game.


Player positions

The 11 players on a team are typically organized into specific roles and formations: Goalkeeper: Defends the goal and is the only player permitted to use their hands within the penalty area. Defenders: Positioned in front of the goalkeeper to stop attacks from the opposing team. Midfielders: Play a crucial role in both offense and defense, linking the defenders and forwards. Forwards (Strikers): Primarily responsible for creating and finishing goal-scoring opportunities.
